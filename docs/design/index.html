Topic: Fractions Addition Missed on: Question 2

Problem: Calculate 2/5 + 1/4

Your Answer: 3/9 | Correct Answer: 13/20
💡 SARA Misconception Breakdown:

You added the numerators (2+1 = 3) and added the denominators (5+4 = 9) directly. This is a classic fractions trap! You can only add fractions once they share a common denominator. For 5 and 4, the lowest common denominator is 20. Convert 2/5 to 8/20, and 1/4 to 5/20. Then add only the top values: 8 + 5 = 13. Answer is 13/20.

🎯 Correcting this: +15 GCSE Mastery Points
Topic: Decimal Arithmetic Missed on: Question 4

Problem: Calculate 1.2 × 0.4

Your Answer: 4.8 | Correct Answer: 0.48
💡 SARA Misconception Breakdown:

Remember, when multiplying two decimal values, count the total number of decimal places in BOTH multiplying numbers. 1.2 (1 place) × 0.4 (1 place) = 2 places total in the final result. Multiply 12 by 4 = 48, then pull the decimal point back 2 places from the right: 0.48.

🎯 Correcting this: +10 GCSE Mastery Points
